About Me

I'm a graduate student at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee in the Leonard E Parker Center for Gravitation, Cosmology and Astrophysics. My research has focused on smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) simulations of white dwarf mergers, as well as data analysis for the detection of gravitational wave signatures in pulsar timing arrays (see my Research page).

Before coming to Milwaukee, I received my BS in physics (minor in mathematics) from the University of Pittsburgh. While an undergraduate there, I analyzed spectral images of galaxies for the DEEP3 survey in order to determine their redshifts and I also worked as a laboratory assistant in condensed matter research.

In addition to physics, I'm also interested in data science and have competed in several Kaggle data science competitions (see my Projects page).
